HVAC emergencies don't wait for convenient times, and neither do we. Our 24/7 emergency HVAC service in Castroville, TX, ensures that help is always available when your heating or cooling system fails. What Constitutes an HVAC Emergency

We consider any situation that threatens your safety, comfort, or property to be an emergency requiring immediate attention. Common HVAC emergencies include complete system failures during extreme weather, gas leaks, electrical problems that pose safety risks, water leaks from HVAC equipment, carbon monoxide detection, and any situation where your system is creating unusual noises or odors that indicate potential safety hazards.

Heating Emergencies

Heating emergencies are particularly serious during Texas's cooler months when outdoor temperatures can drop significantly. Complete heating system failures, gas leaks, carbon monoxide detection, pilot light problems that won't stay lit, and unusual noises or odors from heating equipment all require immediate professional attention to ensure your family's safety and comfort.

Cooling Emergencies

Air conditioning emergencies during Texas's hot summer months can quickly make indoor conditions unbearable. Complete AC failures, refrigerant leaks, frozen coils, electrical problems, and water leaks from cooling equipment all require urgent repair to restore comfortable indoor conditions and prevent additional damage.

Common Emergency Repairs

Our emergency technicians are experienced in handling all types of urgent HVAC problems. Common emergency repairs include compressor failures, blower motor replacements, electrical component failures, gas valve problems, heat exchanger issues, refrigerant leak repairs, and thermostat malfunctions that prevent system operation.

Electrical Emergency Repairs

Electrical problems in HVAC systems can pose safety risks and cause complete system failures. Our emergency electricians can safely diagnose and repair electrical issues including faulty contactors, failed capacitors, wiring problems, and control board failures that prevent proper system operation.

Mechanical Component Failures

Mechanical failures such as compressor breakdowns, blower motor failures, and fan motor problems can cause complete system shutdowns. Our emergency technicians carry replacement motors and other mechanical components for most major brands, allowing us to restore operation quickly.

Safety-First Emergency Protocols

Safety is our top priority during emergency service calls. Our technicians follow strict safety protocols and use professional testing equipment to identify and address any safety concerns. If we detect gas leaks, carbon monoxide, or other safety hazards, we take immediate action to protect your family while working to resolve the problem.

Gas Leak Response

Gas leaks require immediate attention and specific safety protocols. Our emergency technicians are trained in gas leak detection and repair procedures. If a gas leak is suspected, we'll immediately secure the area, shut off gas supply if necessary, and contact appropriate authorities while working to identify and repair the source of the leak.

Carbon Monoxide Detection

Carbon monoxide is a serious safety concern with heating systems. Our emergency technicians carry professional-grade carbon monoxide detectors and are trained to identify and address potential sources of carbon monoxide production. If carbon monoxide is detected, we prioritize immediate safety measures while identifying the cause.

What to Do During an HVAC Emergency

If you experience an HVAC emergency, the first step is to ensure your family's safety. If you smell gas, evacuate immediately and call 911.
